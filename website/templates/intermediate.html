Workout for intermediate

Get started with fitness! These guided workouts aim at helping you start exercising daily with very simple and easy-to-do exercises.

Jumping Jacks

It is a simple, easy-to-do calisthenics workout, which means it’s great for cardiovascular health and boosting your metabolism. Jump training may be good for bone health, too. It increases speed, quickness, and power — and they work your whole body.

Mountain Climber

Mountain climbers are great for building cardio endurance, core strength, and agility. You work several different muscle groups with mountain climbers—it's almost like getting a total-body workout with just one exercise.

Lunges

Lunges are a powerful exercise, allowing you to shape and strengthen almost every muscle in the lower body. It targets your quads, hamstrings, glutes, hips, and calves. This improves their flexibility and counteracts the shortening and tightening while sitting for long periods.

Push-ups

The push-up builds both upper-body and core strength. The upper body muscles that are used in the push-up are the deltoids of the shoulders, the pectoral muscles of the chest, the triceps and biceps of the upper arm, the gluteal or hip muscles, and the erector spinae of the back.

Butt bridge

The basic bridge strengthens your glutes (butt) and hamstrings (back of the thigh) while enhancing core stability. It's easy to add into a strength training workout, can be used as a warm-up, and is even a good rehab exercise for improving core and spinal stabilization.

Wide arm pushups

Wide push-ups are a simple variation of the standard push-up, the primary difference being a wider hand placement. Also known as a wide-grip push-up, this effective exercise activates muscle groups throughout your body.

Child’s pose

Child's Pose (Balasana) is yoga's most important resting posture and it is a nice way to gently stretch various parts of your body. It's a chance to stop what you are doing, reassess your position, reconnect with your breath, and prepare yourself to move forward.
