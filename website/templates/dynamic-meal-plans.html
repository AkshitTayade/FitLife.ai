{% if diet_prediction == 0 %}

You are recommended: Low Carb Diet

A low-carb diet limits carbohydrates, primarily found in sugary foods, pasta, and bread. Instead of eating carbs, you focus on protein-rich whole foods and vegetables. Studies show that low-carb diets can result in weight loss and improved health markers. Best yet, there’s usually no need to count calories or use special products. All you need to do is eat whole foods that make for a complete, nutritious, and filling diet.

{% elif diet_prediction == 1 %}

You are recommended: Balanced Diet

A balanced diet is a diet that contains differing kinds of foods in certain quantities so that the requirement for calories, proteins, minerals, vitamins and alternative nutrients is adequate. A balanced diet should offer around 60-70% of total calories from carbohydrates, 10-12% from proteins and 20-25% of total calories from fat. Healthy eating increases energy, improves the way your body functions, strengthens your immune system and prevents weight gain.

{% elif diet_prediction == 2 %}

You are recommended: Zone Diet

The Zone Diet suggests eating a specific ratio of 40% carbs, 30% protein and 30% fat. It aims to reduce inflammation and encourage healthy insulin levels. It may help people balance their intake of protein and carbohydrate. It includes eating a meal or snack within 1 hour of waking up in the morning. Eat small, frequent meals during the day, every 4–6 hours after a meal or 2–2.5 hours after a snack, whether hungry or not. Drink at least eight 8-ounce glasses of water.

{% elif diet_prediction == 3 %}

You are recommended: Keto Diet

The ketogenic diet is a very low-carb, high-fat diet. To achieve positive results, this diet has to be very low in carbohydrates, high in dietary fat and include a moderate amount of proteins. This reduction in carbs puts your body into a metabolic state called ketosis. When this happens, your body becomes incredibly efficient at burning fat for energy. Ketogenic diet can cause massive reductions in blood sugar, insulin levels and help with weight loss.

{% elif diet_prediction == 4 %}

You are recommended: Depletion Diet

Carb depletion diets are diets that restrict either all dietary carbohydrates or certain types of carbohydrates. Diets that restrict carbohydrates to an absolute minimum deplete the body’s stores of glucose, the building blocks of carbohydrates, within 12 to 14 hours. After the body’s stores of glucose have been depleted, the body starts to burn fat. When this happens, your body becomes incredibly efficient at burning fat for energy.

{% else %}

You are recommended: High Carb Diet

Healthy high-carb diets can have carbohydrate content that ranges from around 64 percent of your daily diet to 85 percent of your diet. These diets have been shown to promote weight loss, reverse liver disease and reduce health problems when consumed long term. They typically focus on the consumption of low-fat, high-carb foods like complex carbohydrates and fiber-rich vegetables and fruits.
